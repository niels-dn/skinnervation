Guide

The nerve index covers four anatomical regions. Select a region using the filter buttons in the toolbar to explore its cutaneous innervation.

Regions
Head / Neck Cranial nerves V, VII and X
Spinal nerves C2–C4
Trigeminal, facial and vagal cutaneous branches, together with the cervical plexus supplying the posterior scalp, neck and upper shoulder.
Upper limb Spinal nerves C5–T1
Brachial plexus derivatives covering the entire arm, forearm, and hand, including medial cutaneous, musculocutaneous, radial, median and ulnar contributions.
Truncus Spinal nerves T2–T11
Intercostal nerves and their lateral and anterior cutaneous branches, forming segmental dermatomal bands across the thorax and abdomen.
Lower body Spinal nerves T12–Co
Lumbar, sacral and coccygeal plexus branches supplying the lower abdomen, perineum, gluteal region, and the entire lower limb.

Skinnervation presents cutaneous innervation in two complementary views. Use the toggle in the toolbar to switch between them.

Pathway View

The Pathway View shows the complete hierarchical structure of peripheral nerve innervation as a network diagram, from spinal root to terminal cutaneous branch. Nodes are arranged left to right by anatomical level: spinal roots, plexuses, trunks, and terminal branches.

Browsing

Use the region filter buttons — Head/Neck, Upper limb, Truncus, Lower body — to display one body region at a time. You can also search by nerve name or anatomical index number to jump directly to any nerve.

Highlighting a pathway

Click any node to highlight its full innervation pathway — upstream toward the spinal cord and downstream toward the skin. The info panel shows the nerve's full anatomical name, spinal origin, and index number.

Dashed borders

A dashed border on a node or edge indicates variable innervation — a contribution present in some individuals but not all. These are shown in context when a pathway is highlighted.

Anatomic View

The Anatomic View shows the approximate cutaneous territories of upper limb nerves overlaid on a schematic of the arm, seen from the anterior and posterior aspects.

Switching to Anatomic View

When a terminal cutaneous nerve of the upper limb is selected in Pathway View, an Anatomic View button appears in the info panel. Clicking it switches to the Anatomic View with the selected nerve in focus.

Interacting in Anatomic View

Click any nerve label in the Anatomic View to open its info panel. A Pathway View button in the panel returns you to the Pathway View with that nerve highlighted and its pathway traced.

Territories

The territories shown are approximations of typical innervation patterns. Significant individual variation exists, particularly at territory borders. They should be interpreted in the context of the clinical examination.
