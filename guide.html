Guide

Browse

Use the region filter buttons to show one body region at a time: Head/Neck, Upper limb, Truncus, or Lower body. Click any node to highlight its pathway from spinal root to terminal cutaneous branch.

Trace a nerve

Clicking a node highlights the complete innervation pathway upstream (toward the spinal cord) and downstream (toward the skin). The info panel shows the nerve's full name, spinal origin, and anatomical index number.

Dashed lines

A dashed border on a node or edge indicates variable innervation — a contribution that is present in some individuals but not all. These are shown in the highlighted pathway when relevant.

Anatomic view

For upper limb nerves, the Anatomic View button in the info panel switches to a schematic of the arm showing the approximate cutaneous territory of the selected nerve. Click Pathway View to return.

Limitations

Skinnervation represents typical innervation patterns based on standard anatomical sources. Significant individual variation exists in cutaneous territories, particularly at territory borders. The anatomic territories shown are approximations and should be interpreted in the context of the clinical examination.
