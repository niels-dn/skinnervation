Guide

The nerve index covers the cutaneous innervation of the entire human body, which is divided in four anatomical regions. Select a region using the filter buttons in the toolbar to explore its cutaneous innervation.

Regions
Head / Neck Cranial nerves V, VII and X
Spinal nerves C2–C4
Trigeminal, facial and vagal cutaneous branches, together with the cervical plexus supplying the posterior scalp, neck and upper shoulder.
Upper limb Spinal nerves C5–T1
Brachial plexus derivatives covering the entire arm, forearm, and hand, including medial cutaneous, musculocutaneous, radial, median and ulnar contributions.
Truncus Spinal nerves T2–T11
Intercostal nerves and their lateral and anterior cutaneous branches, forming segmental dermatomal bands across the thorax and abdomen.
Lower body Spinal nerves T12–Co
Lumbar, sacral and coccygeal plexus branches supplying the lower abdomen, perineum, gluteal region, and the entire lower limb.

Skinnervation presents cutaneous innervation in two complementary views, toggled in the toolbar.

Pathway View

The Pathway View shows the hierarchical structure of peripheral nerve innervation as a network diagram — from spinal root to terminal cutaneous branch — for one body region at a time.

Browsing

Use the region buttons — Head/Neck, Upper limb, Truncus, Lower body — or search by nerve name or index number to navigate directly to any nerve.

Interaction

Click any node to highlight its full innervation pathway, upstream toward the spinal cord and downstream toward the skin. The info panel shows the nerve's full anatomical name, spinal origin, and index number. A dashed border on a node or edge indicates variable innervation — present in some individuals but not all.

Anatomic View

The Anatomic View shows the approximate cutaneous territories of peripheral nerves overlaid on anatomical outlines of the relevant body region, seen from the anterior and posterior aspects.

Switching views

When a terminal cutaneous nerve is selected in Pathway View, an Anatomic View button appears in the info panel. Clicking it switches to Anatomic View with the selected nerve in focus.

Interaction

Click any nerve label to open its info panel. A Pathway View button in the panel returns you to Pathway View with that nerve highlighted.

Territories

The territories shown are approximations. Significant individual variation exists, particularly at borders, and should be interpreted in a clinical context.
